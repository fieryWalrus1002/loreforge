Robots
The vast majority of bots in the zone function as ex-
pert system bots, competent within their function but
lacking any true general intelligence or self-awareness.
Whenever a bot is called on to function outside its role,
make a skill check using the bot’s skill modifier; if it
fails, it tries to use its usual behavior even if such a
behavior is inappropriate.
Initial bot reactions will depend on their intended
role. If programmed to attack their initial response
will always be hostile, but other bots will react accord-
ing to the Reaction Roll. The default response will
be an attempt to behave as its programming dictates.
Robots are self-powered and do not normally run
out of ammunition for any weapons they have built in.
Robot Types
Brainbot: “Surplus” human neural tissue was salvaged
to give these human-like bots a degree of inde-
pendent intelligence in order to direct their sim-
pler brethren. Long neglect and inadequate main-
tenance has driven most of them quite insane.
Clanker: These simple maintenance and labor bots are
usually harmless, but a malicious programmer
can convert them to minimally-adequate fight-
ers. They are incapable of using weapons unless
programmed to do so.
Hellrider: Servants of the Territorial Rangers, these
centauroid robots fight with a pair of integral
pistols that have an effective range of 100 me-
ters. They are skilled trackers, and a posse of
2d6 of them inevitably accompany any ranger.
Lone hellriders or small groups of 1d6 of them
are sometimes found near Mandate installations,
conducting endless patrols.
Iron Horse: Chrome-maned equibots built to carry
dudes who might not know how to sit a real horse,
many of these bots have survived to bond with
the lucky rider who can find their control cards
or override their rental programming.
Janglesnake: A corroded iron snake that burrows be-
neath the dust, a janglesnake’s vibroblade teeth
once flowed with a venom that has long since
run dry. After a successful bite, however, a jan-
glesnake gets a free constriction grapple attempt
against the victim with a +3 total skill bonus.
Juggernaut: Fearsome chariots of doom, a tank-sized
juggernaut is normally found only within the Al-
buquerque city core or guarding high-level Man-
date facilities. It can run over creatures as part
of its Move actions, forcing an Evasion save or
instant death. Its onboard weapons have a 1,000
meter range.
Scorpoid: These wolf-sized metal scorpions often
appear in packs of 1d4+1, skittering across the
sands of the zone. They attack with two mono-
blade claw attacks and one electrostatic “stinger”
zap with a range of 10 meters. Only the claws do
Shock damage.
Scrabbler: One of a host of half-wrecked robots and
malfunctioning machines, these limping wrecks
are animated only by their undying purpose,
which often happens to be mayhem. If not a sad,
lone wreck, they are found in groups of 2d6.
Tinman: These androids were built to resemble hu-
mans, some of them with unsettling accuracy.
Most were intended for human interaction of
some sort, so they are among the most likely ro-
bots to engage peacefully with modern survivors.
Even so, they remain mere expert systems, and
will often try to apply their original program-
ming even when it is inappropriate.
Vultron: Most flying robots were wiped out by the
Bright Mirror defense system, but a few mod-
els had acceptable verification codes. The buz-
zard-like vultron was one of them, hovering with
its black-feathered grav units and spitting lethal
laser bolts at prey below. A vultron never flies
higher than 30 meters above the ground.
